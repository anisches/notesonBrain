# Brain

Research notes on the brain, mind, cognition, consciousness, agency, emotion, behavior, and neuroscience.

This repo is for slow research, not quick takes. Every claim should be traceable to a source, and every source should be classified by quality before it is treated as evidence.

## Working Rules

- Separate peer-reviewed research from essays, commentary, podcasts, and Reddit leads.
- Prefer primary papers and review papers over second-hand summaries.
- Track uncertainty clearly: strong evidence, mixed evidence, speculative, or lead only.
- Keep Reddit and web searches as discovery trails, not proof.
- When a source is from PubMed/NCBI, identify the original journal and paper context instead of treating NCBI as the publisher.

## Structure

- `sources/` - individual source notes and compiled bibliographies
- `notes/` - topic notes and synthesis
- `reading-lists/` - prioritized reading queues
- `research-questions/` - open questions to investigate
- `reddit/` - Reddit discovery leads and discussion summaries
- `neuro/` - brain regions, circuits, neurotransmitters, and methods
- `psychology/` - cognition, behavior, affect, decision-making
- `philosophy/` - mind, self, agency, free will, identity
- `methods/` - how to search, classify, and evaluate sources
- `daily/` - dated work logs

## Source Quality Labels

- `primary-paper` - original peer-reviewed research
- `review` - peer-reviewed review, meta-analysis, or systematic review
- `book` - academic or serious long-form source
- `preprint` - unpublished or not yet peer-reviewed
- `commentary` - essay, editorial, interview, talk, podcast, blog
- `reddit-lead` - useful discovery lead only
- `wiki-reference` - orientation only, not evidence
